Learn about Alopecia Areata

Alopecia Areata is a type of disease that causes extreme hair loss, which can result in going bald in less than a week! Alopecia begins with small patches in the hair to fall, which can increase exponentially and cause rapid and severe hair loss. Alopecia Areata is a threatening problem that about 1 in 50 people are suffering with, sometimes lasting over a year!

However, we can promote awareness of Alopecia Areata and increase the treatments for it tremendously!

Alopecia Areata

Alopecia Areata is an autoimmune disease, meaning that the immune system attacks a part of the body mistakenly. When infected with Alopecia, cells in the immune system surround and destroy large groups of hair follicles, which leads to hair loss in small patches on the head.

Alopecia is often caused due to a deficiency in Vitamin D, as it weakens the immune system and causes the loss of hair follicles. There are several ways to tell if you are infected by Alopecia Areata, and how you can prevent yourself and others from contracting it. Although Alopecia can cause full hair loss in less than a month, there are often ways to naturally regrow the hair follicles with the help of topical and oral medications.

An autoimmune disease makes it so the body's natural defense system is unable to tell the difference between its own cells and foreign cells, causing the body to mistakenly attack healthy cells. Alopecia Areata falls in this group, which is why it is hard to find a guaranteed treatment. Most autoimmune diseases, including Alopecia, can generally affect many types of tissues and nearly any organ in your body, making it hard to treat efficiently. For example, with Alopecia Areata, you might get severe hair loss in all parts of the body, which will make it difficult to fully regrow.

Symptoms and Causes

Symptoms

  • Small patches of hair on your scalp falling rapidly, creating numerous bald spots.

  • Losing significantly more hair than normal in a short period of time. The average hair loss is about 50-200 hair strands per day.

  • Losing more hair during cold temperatures, or in dry environments.

  • Mainly losing hair on the top of your scalp, slower on the sides of the head.

  • Fingernails or toenails become slightly brittle or easy to break, sometimes, break on their own.

  • Feel a slight itch or burn when the hair patches are about to fall.

Causes & Risk Factors

  • Since Alopecia is an autoimmune disease, generally the main cause is genetics and hormones. This means there is a higher chance of contracting it if one of your family members had it at some point.

  • Poor nutriton and lack of vitamins is another major cause to severe Alopecia Areata. This is because compared to a stronger immune system, a poor immune system will attack more healthy cells in the body.

  • High levels of stress can increase the rate at which Alopecia spreads in the body, making it more severe. This is because it causes your hair to thin overtime, mostly due to the mental state that your body is in.

  • Alopecia will increase exponentially, meaning the more you see that your hair is falling, the faster your chance of going bald is. Fortunately, patients on treatment have drastically reduced hair loss compared to patients with no treatment.

Treatment

Since Alopecia is an autoimmune disease, there is not a 100% cure, however there are ways to reduce hair loss.
Hover over each image below to learn more about the various treatment options that are available.
Corticosteroids
One major treatment for Alopecia Areata is the use of corticosteroids. This will supress the immune system and stop it from attacking hair follicles, which will lower the rate of hair loss. However, there are some side effects with corticosteroids, including the chance of high blood pressure, problems with mood swings, and muscle/body weakness.
Topical Immunotherapy
Another treatment for Alopecia Areata is Topical Immunotherapy. It involves applying chemicals including, diphencyprone and dinitrochlorobenzene, directly on the scalp to cause a reaction to the immune system and help regrow hair follicles.
Topical Minoxidil
Alopecia Areata can also be treated with the use of Topical Minoxidil. It works by boosting hair growth after the immune system is no longer attacking the hair follicles. Topical Minoxidil does not work for extensive hair loss, however, it is good for regrowing small hair follicles.
Topical Anthralin
Topical Anthralin can be used to treat mild Alopecia Areata. It creates a reaction to cause hair growth. However, there is a risk that the immune system attacks the medicine, causing the hair follicles to fall quicker. It is recommended to use small amounts of Topical Anthralin on the hair.
Rogaine
If there are small bald spots caused by Alopecia Areata, Rogaine can be used. It works by first removing the hair on the applied spot, then, creates new hair follicles to replace the dead ones. It usually takes about 12 weeks before the hair starts to regrow.
Platelet-rich Plasma
Platelet-rich Plasma is a way to speed up the healing in parts of your body. When applied on the hair, small hair follicles that were not affected by Alopecia will grow faster, and thus, create more hair.
